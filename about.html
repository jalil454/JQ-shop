— The idea

Clothes are the first thing the world sees. Make them say something worth hearing.

JQ started with a sketchbook and an obsession. Ornamental art — the kind you find carved into old buildings, tattooed into skin, drawn into margins — translated onto fabric. Not as decoration. As identity.

We are not a fashion brand. We are a statement brand. Every piece we release carries a mark — the JQ emblem — and with it, a philosophy: that what you wear should be a deliberate choice, not a default.

We drop when the work is ready. We don't chase trends. We don't restock. When it's gone, it's gone. That's not scarcity for scarcity's sake — it's respect for the craft.

— How we got here

The Journey

2025
The Sketchbook

It started with drawings. Pages of ornamental logos, fabric experiments, and the question: what would streetwear look like if it took visual art seriously?

Early 2026
First Samples

The first JQ pieces came off the press. Heavyweight cotton, embroidered emblems, no compromise on quality. Friends wore them. Strangers asked where to buy them.

2026
JQ Launches

The brand goes live. Three categories — T-Shirts, Hoodies, Pants — each with the JQ mark. Limited runs. No restocks. The first drop sells out in 72 hours.

Next
What Comes Next

More drops. More art. Collaborations with artists who think the way we do. JQ is only getting started.

— What drives us

Our Values

01
Craft First

Every garment starts with the question: is this the best version of this piece? If the answer is no, it doesn't ship. Quality is not a feature — it's the baseline.

02
Limited Always

We release in limited runs. Not to manufacture hype — to guarantee that what you hold is rare by design. When a drop is gone, it belongs to those who moved for it.

03
Art Over Trend

We don't follow seasons. We follow ideas. The JQ aesthetic is rooted in ornamental art history — timeless shapes that don't expire when the algorithm moves on.

— How it's made

The Process

01
Concept

Every piece starts as a drawing. Ornamental motifs, logo placements, fabric weight — all decided before a single sample is made.

02
Sample

We produce multiple samples and wear-test them. If the fabric doesn't feel right after washing, the print doesn't hold — we go back to start.

03
Produce

Approved samples go into limited production. Every run is numbered. Every piece is quality-checked before it ships.

04
Drop

When the pieces are ready, we drop. No pre-orders. No countdowns. The date is announced 48 hours before. First come, first get.
